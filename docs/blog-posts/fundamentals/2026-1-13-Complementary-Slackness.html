Complementary Slackness in Semi-definite Programming: Equivalent Inner Product or Matrix Condition

fundamentals
matrix-analysis
optimization
Author

Ian McPherson

Published

January 7, 2026

ABSTRACT Within this post we provide the elementary proof for the equivalence of the inner product and matrix forms of the complementary slackness condition in semidefinite programming.

The standard primal form of a semidefinite program (SDP) is given by

\[ \begin{aligned} \min_{X \in \mathcal{S}^{n}} & \quad \langle C, X \rangle \\ \text{s.t.} & \quad \mathcal{A}(X) = b, \quad X \succeq 0, \end{aligned} \]

where the \(\mathbb{S}^{n}\) denotes the space of \(n\)-by-\(n\) symmetric matrices, \(\mathcal{A} \colon \mathbb{S}^{n} \to \mathbb{R}^{m}\) is a linear operator, \(C \in \mathbb{S}^{n}\) is a cost matrix, and \(b \in \mathbb{R}^{m}\) is the constraint vector. We endow \(\mathbb{S}^{n}\) with the trace inner product \(\langle A, B \rangle = \operatorname{trace}(A B)\) for \(A, B \in \mathbb{S}^{n}\).

The corresponding Fenchel dual problem is given by

\[ \begin{aligned} \max_{y \in \mathbb{R}^{m}} & \quad \langle b, y \rangle \\ \text{s.t.} & \quad C - \mathcal{A}^{*}(y) \succeq 0, \end{aligned} \]

For simplicity, we denote the dual slack by \(Z_{y} := C - \mathcal{A}^{*}(y)\) for \(y \in \mathbb{R}^{m}\).

We consider the complementary slackness condition in semidefinite programming (SDP), which is well-known to be equivalent to strong duality. Namely, if \(X^{\star}\) and \(y^{\star}\) are optimal solutions to the primal and dual SDP problems respectively, then the complementary slackness can be expressed in two equivalent forms:

  1. Inner Product Form: \(\langle Z_{y^{\star}}, X^{\star} \rangle_{F} = 0\).

  2. Matrix Form: \(Z_{y^{\star}} X^{\star} = 0\).

For the rest of this post, we provide a simple proof of the equivalence of these two forms.

Proof of Equivalence

To show the equivalence, we will prove both directions:

(1) Inner Product Form \(\implies\) Matrix Form
